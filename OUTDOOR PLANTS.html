Water the plant when the top 1 to 1 inch of soil feels dry to the touch. Ficus elastica prefer consistent moisture, but it is necessary to let the soil dry out slightly between watering to prevent overwatering and root rot.

Yucca gigantea should be watered every few weeks during the active growing season between spring and summer, and less frequently in the fall and winter months. Excessive watering can be detrimental to this species. Yellowing or browning of the lower leaves is a sign of overwatering. Allow the soil to dry completely between waterings to prevent root rot.

In normal time, a mist of water can be sprayed towards the leaves to increase the surrounding humidity and prevent burrs Leaves from drying out. It should be noted that the dracaena angustifolia plant is sensitive to fluoride in tap water and dark spots or yellow edges may form on its leaves when tap water is used over the long term, so it is recommended to use collected rainwater or purified water.

Ficus benjamina should be watered regularly between spring and fall during the active growing season. Overwatering and underwatering can cause the leaves of this species to fall to the ground. It should be planted in a well-drained pot. Allow the soil to dry out slightly between waterings to prevent overwatering and root rot.
